Dolls Kill

Mobile Web Checkout Process

Summary

Dolls Kill is a fashion ecommerce. This project is a redesign of their checkout process for mobile web. My main goal was to create a checkout process that was both easy to access as well as quickly completed. Because I was designing for mobile web, I decided to cut out some features that I would normally include for tablet or desktop. I tried to keep the entire website as easily navigable as possible by minimizing the number of screens the user has to go through to checkout, as well as keeping the user oriented through visible indications of their location. I kept the fonts large and readable and the styles consistent.

Navigation

Sticky Nav Bar

A hamburger menu replaces the direct navigation everywhere except on the landing screen where a couple categories give the user some direction where to start looking. The sticky menu allows for easy access to the cart from anywhere on the site. It also allows easy access to login, your account information, favorites, search bar and allows you to navigate easily around the site with minimal scrolling.

Shopping Cart Design

shopping cart selected
Clickable Cart Select

When selected, your cart appears in a rolled-down modal. When clicked again, your cart rolls back up. You end up in the same location as before you took a look at your cart. In mobile web, there can often be a lot of scrolling and this style of cart accounts for that.

Concise Order Summary and Checkout Access

This allows you to go to checkout directly if you don’t want to scroll through your cart. Standard shipping is default.

Editable Items Summary

Quantities can be changed and items can be deleted here. I chose not to be able to edit anything else directly in the cart to reduce mistakes and simplify the experience. To edit the color or size, you have to click on the picture or title, which are both linked to the item page. (When clicked, the cart rolls up again and the user is navigated to that page).

Checkout plus Paypal Option

Adding a PayPal option here allows users to purchase fast without having to log in to Dolls Kill, or fill out any additional information. It’s not included at the top of the cart because I wanted the users to review their items before this option became available.

Guest Customer Option

I chose not to make users who don’t have an account sign up to be able to make a purchase. Mobile users especially do not want to take this extra step and are likely to not pull the trigger on an order if they’re forced to do so.

Accordian Style Set-up

Setting up the steps as accordian tabs instead of new pages allows users to see how many steps they have left in the process, as well as keeps them on one page which is a lot more orienting than flipping through multiple pages.

Editable Tabs

The completed tabs are collapsed, but can be edited and expanded again when clicked. Non-editable tabs aren’t expandable until you’ve completed the prior steps. Showing the next steps lets the user know how far along in the process they are.

Optional Account Creation

Users have the option of saving their shipping and billing information, but it’s not mandatory

Prepopulated Information

This information is either saved from past orders in a user’s account, pulled from PayPal or filled out in the steps preceding arriving at this page.

Uneditable Fields

To edit your information, you must press the “edit” button, which takes you either to your cart, or your account settings where you can change or add new preset options (in the case of shipping and payment).
