intro

Background

Found Eats is an online marketplace for specialty foods where users can browse the latest products , discover the newest food makers, and purchase their favorite artisanal foods. Food makers looking to sell their products are directed to a vendor-facing website where they can set-up their company to become an active vendor on the online marketplace. After the vendor set-up is complete, this website will be used to manage incoming orders, update product or company details and coordinate product shipping and payment. This project is an optimized redesign of the current Found Eats Vendor Account website meant to fix the existing problems and to enhance the user experience.

ux research

Understanding the User

The first step was getting to know the vendors that would be using the site. I familiarized myself with their stories, daily routines, and the role that they hoped Found Eats would play in their businesses.

Use Cases

  • 1

    I am a new vendor who is going through the set-up process the first time.

  • 2

    I am a vendor who is already up and live on the site, and am visiting the site to modify my information or products.

  • 3

    I am a live vendor checking my account to maintain the orders that come in through the site.

My redesign centers mostly around fixing the problems created by the current design in Use Case #1. As a result, Use Case #2 and Use Case #3 are enhanced.

strategy

My Vision

context

The new Found Eats Vendor Account Website should feel natural and easy to use, and cohesive with the Found Eats customer marketplace. The overall business objective is to design a website that requires minimal maintenance, reduces the amount of errors, and increases the rate by which new vendors successfully complete set-up. The resulting product allows Found Eats to handle large influxes of new vendors while reducing the staff budget.


creating a short, direct path to completion

The design will direct new vendors down a short, direct path to completing their Vendor Account Set-up. Creating this clear, unambiguous trajectory should make the user feel confident in their ability to easily complete the set-up process. Relative to the short process, each information field bears great significance, making it likely that vendors will answer each question diligently and fully. Additionally, the design focuses on creating more clarity with an intuitive layout, and accessible explanations that rely largely on visual content.


building excitement

Perhaps the largest aspect of my vision for the new Found Eats Vendor Account Website is that it promotes an emotional response. It is important that the Vendor Account website is not only beautiful, but also reminiscent of the Found Eats marketplace. This provides context for users undergoing the set-up process, and reminds them of the end goal, to become a live vendor on Found Eats. As they see their company profiles coming together, vendors recall the experiences that have led them to become small food makers . Often the company stories include highly personal motivations like passing down grandma’s recipe or coming up with creations for a family member with dietary restrictions. My optimized Vendor Account Website is designed to not only elicit strong emotions like nostalgia, warmth or pride, but also provide a means to express them by uploading pictures and sharing experiences on their company profile. What is currently a daunting but necessary chore to sign-up and maintain your company on the Found Eats Vendor Account website, will suddenly not only seem simple and clear, but will be transformed into an opportunity for you to share your story, show your passion, and communicate your product and personality with a large community of specialty food lovers.

broad solutions

Organizational Changes

  1. Reduced Number of Sections

    2. I condensed the number of sections on the site, grouping the sections that thematically worked together to create more unity within a fewer number of steps.

  2. Reduced number of clickaway pages

    3. I reduced the number of clickaway pages, by allowing the user to edit information directly on pages previously used only for the organization of many separate pages. Accordian tabs make it possible to manage a large amount of information in a condensed and organized fashion.

page-specific changes

Dashboard

When a user first logs into their account, they land on this dashboard. Currently, this page is the main page meant to be used for navigation during the set-up process since it shows the users progress and set-up trajectory. The problem with the current layout is that once the set-up is complete, the Dashboard does not convey any important information for a live vendor.

In this first section, I am redesigning the whole navigation system of the site upon first logging in. I am repurposing the Dashboard altogether and dispersing the components of its current design into other structural fixtures in the site.

Problems
  1. This progress bar is the only feature on the whole site that indicates what needs to be filled out and what has been adequately filled out. Consequently, a user must go back to this page everytime they want to make sure a section has been completed. This bar also makes the process of setting up very daunting with many steps.
  2. This infographic is highlighting a step that is unrelated to any of the steps to set-up on this vendor account website. It is in a place of great prominence.
  3. This navigation bar does not delineate between sections of the set-up process, and sections that will be used once the vendor is up and live.
  4. This whole section contains video walkthroughs of each section in the set-up process. These videos do not appear on the actual pages that they are corresponded with, therefore vendors are not watching them.

Solutions
  1. Making the progress bar a general site-wide feature so that it appears on every page.
  2. Taking out the infographic from the site altogether and instead putting it in an automated email sent to new vendors.
  3. The new navigation bar separates the set-up process from live order management. The whole Dashboard set-up serves this purpose as well.
  4. The walkthrough video tutorials are now dropped into the pages that they are relevent to. (The information in the walk-through is also given in written form since people are not as inclined to watch videos even if they see it as an option.)

Company Page

Most of the problems on this page are due to misunderstanding what information is being asked, and misunderstanding the purpose for uploading this information.

These problems have been solved by changing the format to mimic the customer-facing side of the site and by the addition of element-by-element descriptions on the right hand side fixed panel.

Problems
  1. Nobody was clicking on “Preview Company Page” and therefore getting the benefits of seeing what their page would look like live.
  2. Users did not understand what a banner photo meant and even if they did, without any context, it was difficult for them to choose a picture that would look good and fit the dimensions.
  3. Vendors were rushing through this section or putting only one or two words here because they did not understand the purpose of what they entered.
  4. There was a change in Shipping Policy- the solutions to this are shown on the individual product pages.
  5. The set-up here is strange. It’s unclear what is being asked and it is unclear what “Heading” or “Description” means.

Solutions
  1. 1. By formatting the entire page like the live pages, this eliminates the need to add a “preview” button altogether.
  2. Seeing the banner photo formatted like this allows the user to adjust their own photo to the dimensions on the page instead of having to formatting it before uploading it. It also allows the user to see what the photo will look like and help pick one that would work best.
  3. Showing that the description will be something that customers see will improve the quality of vendors’ inputs.
  4. Seeing how gallery items are formatted on live pages helps the user understand what they should upload here, and the importance of filling their company page with photos and videos.

Making the main product page mimic the customer- facing side of the site makes it much clearer what is live and what is still in draft form. Variants are easily defined.

The new design will provide clarity to the users as well as evoke an emotional response that will compel a potential vendor to finish the set-up process to become a live vendor.
